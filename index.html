Abstract

Diffusion models have gained popularity for generating content in various domains, including text, image, and video. In particular, Text-to-Image(T2I) models have emerged as an innovative tool for artistic expression. They also spurred growth in other associated areas, such as personalized subject generation and one-shot video editing. This paper presents VideoDreamBooth, a novel framework for real video editing with personalized subject. Prior video editing methods are mostly mask-based that determines which area of the original video to be edited and which not. The masks used in these methods are either provided by users or obtained from cross attention maps during denoising process. The former has limited application scenarios due to its labeling cost. The latter may harm temporal consistency at pixel level, since cross attention maps are obtained in the latent space. VideoDreamBooth addresses these limitations by using a novel mask-free paradigm. Inspired by the coarse-to-fine denoising nature of diffusion, we propose a multi-path fusion technique to preserve temporal consistency with original video. We also propose a metric $CLIP score$ to guide which denoising steps will conduct fusion. Our framework has proven to be effective and successful for video editing in various experimental settings. Our code and visualized results are attached to the supplementary.

Pipeline

Left Top, Stage1: we fine-tune a T2I model to learn personalized concepts.

Left Bottom, Stage2: we inflates the fine-tuned T2I model to a T2V model with an additional temporal attention module to preserve temporal consistency from source video.

Right, Stage3: we invert the source video to a deterministic Gaussian noise through null-text inversion as shown in stage 3(a). Finally, we fuse the inversion path in Stage2 and generation path guided by target prompt to generate our personalized concept in source video.
